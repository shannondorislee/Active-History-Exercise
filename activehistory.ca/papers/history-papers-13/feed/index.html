Resistance to Residential Schools: Digital Stories

As part of a “Resistance Narratives” project funded by the Truth and Reconciliation Commission of Canada, the Centre for Youth & Society hired seven Indigenous youth research assistants to create digital stories documenting resistance to the residential school system.

The project, “Residential School Resistance Narratives: Significance and Strategies for Indigneous Youth,” aimed to increase awareness of resistance acts and strategies, and their relevance in today’s context and promote intergenerational healing.

Project coordinator Asma Antoine believes that “this research project has ignited a fire that shows in each digital story. The passion of resistance that validates survival and resilience of First Nations people and communities provides hope for healing and reconciliation over the next generations.”

These videos were presented to family, friends, and guests at UVic and then to a greater public audience as part of the TRC regional conference in April.
